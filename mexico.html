Mexico: Raspado


The Mexican version of shaved ice is called a raspado, which comes from the Spanish word "to scrape". Typically, a raspado is topped with fresh fruit, syrup, and milk, and is served in a cup with a straw or spoon. What makes raspado unique is the usage of ingredients such as tamarind, rompope (eggnog), or spicy sauce.

Raspado Recipe

Ingredient Amount
Strawberries cut into quarters 1 pound
Sugar 1 cup
Water 2 1/2 cups
Ice As needed

Steps

  1. Add sugar and water in a saucepan. Mix to dissolve.
  2. Boil for 5 minutes.
  3. Add the strawberries and simmer for 20 minutes.
  4. Allow to cool down then transfer to a jar and chill in the fridge for 2 hours.
  5. Fill your blender about halfway with ice cubes.
  6. Pulse a few times on high until all ice has a shaved consistency.
  7. Transfer shaved ice to cups and pack it in.
  8. Pour over some syrup, and top with fruit bites.
  9. Serve with a straw and a spoon.
