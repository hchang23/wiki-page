Hawaii: Shave Ice


In the mid-1800s, Japanese immigrants who came to Hawaii to work on plantations brought with them kakigori, which grew in popularity. The immigrants would use their tools to get shaved ice from large blocks of ice and then adding sugar or fruit juice, which is easily absorbed by the ice due to it being finely shaved like powder. Fresh, local fruits such as coconut, papaya, or passionfruit are used as toppings or fuit syrups/juice; ice cream is usually added at the core; and adzuki beans are a common topping.

Hawaiian Shave Ice Recipe

Ingredient Amount
Frozen mango pulp, thawed 2/3 cup
Sugar 1/3 cup
Raspberry jam 1 cup
Hot water 1/2 cup and 2 tablespoons
Sweetened condensed milk 1/2 cup
Ice As needed

Steps

  1. Heat the sugar and 1/3 cup water in a small saucepan until it comes to a simmer and the sugar dissolves.
  2. Turn off the heat and let cool completely.
  3. Whisk in the mango pulp, then transfer to a squeeze bottle and set aside.
  4. Meanwhile, whisk the jam together in a bowl with the hot water.
  5. Allow to cool completely. Transfer to a squeeze bottle and set aside.
  6. Transfer the condensed milk to a squeeze bottle.
  7. In a food processor that's turned on, drop the ice cubes in one by one until they have a snow-like consistency. Remove any large chunks.
  8. Pile the shaved ice into dishes. Squeeze one syrup over half of the dish of ice and the remaining ice with the other syrup.
  9. Drizzle with the sweetened condensed milk to taste.
  10. Repeat with the remaining ice and syrups and condensed milk.
