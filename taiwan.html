Taiwan: Baobing/Tsuabing


Shaved ice is very famous in Taiwan and can be easily found on the streets or in night markets. Traditional ways of preparing baobing is shaving a pile of ice and covering it in multiple toppings such as red beans, mung beans, glutinous rice balls, tapiocal pearls, taro cubes, grass jelly, and condensed milk. Fruits are also a common topping, with mango being a favorite option.

Baobing Recipe

Ingredient Amount
Condensed milk As desired
Adzuki beans 1/2 cup
Water 4 cups
Sugar 4 tablespoons
Dried mung beans 1/2 cup
Grass jelly 1 can or packet
Taro, sweet potato balls, boba pearls, fresh fruit, pudding, etc. As desired
Ice As needed

Steps

Prepare sweetened red beans and mung bean (instant pot method)
  1. Wash the adzuki beans.
  2. Add the washed beans and 2 cups of water to the Instant Pot.
  3. Cook at high pressure for 25 minutes and wait at least 10 minutes before natural releasing.
  4. Stir in 2 tablespoons sugar while the beans are still hot so that the sugar melts.
  5. Repeat with mung beans, but pressure cook on high pressure for 8 minutes and natural release for 10.
Prepare sweetened red beans and mung beans (stovetop method)
  1. First, soak the beans in separate bowls overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked beans, transfer them to separate pots, and add the water.
  3. Reduce the heat and simmer until the beans are tender (~1 hour for adzuki beans and 20-30 minutes for mung beans).
  4. Mix in 2 tablespoons of sugar each and set aside to cool.
Toppings and assembling
  1. Cook the tapioca pearls/boba according to packaging instructions.
  2. Cook the taro and sweet potato balls.
  3. Prepare other desired toppings, such as cut fruit.
  4. Use a shaved ice machine (manual or electric) to make shaved ice. You can also use a food processor/high-speed blender to pulverize ice cubes into flakes.
  5. Place the shaved ice into a large bowl. Add the prepared toppings on top.
  6. Finally, drizzle the condensed milk on top and serve immediately.
