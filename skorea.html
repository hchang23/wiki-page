South Korea: Bingsoo


Korean shaved ice, called bingsoo, is becoming widely popular across the world due to the huge variety of flavors and toppings. The ice itself is made from frozen cream or milk, and traditional toppings include red bean, bean paste, and tteok (rice cake). Flavors such as green tea, coffee, mango, chocolate, and lychee are a favorite among many.

Patbingsoo Recipe

Ingredient Amount
Whole milk 3 cups
Heavy or light cream (optional) 1/4 cup
Sugar 3 tablespoons
Sweetened red beans 1 can (18 oz)
Chopped sweet rice cakes (optional) 1/2 cup
Sweet condensed milk 4-6 tablespoons

Steps

  1. In a bowl whisk together milk, cream (if using) and sugar until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Pour into a gallon size zip bag and lay flat in the freezer. Freeze it for at least 3 hrs.
  3. Take the bag out of freezer and thaw in on the counter for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Using a rolling pin, pound the bag to break up the solid milk into large chunks flipping the bag back and forth.
  5. When you see the milk broken into large slush chunks, stop the pounding.
  6. Scoop the milk chunks into the individual serving bowls.
  7. Top with red beans, about 3-4 tablespoonfuls per serving, and rice cakes if using.
  8. Drizzle with sweet condensed milk, about 1-2 tablespoon each. Serve immediately.
